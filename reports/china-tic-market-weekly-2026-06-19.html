Executive Summary (for PM / Business Teams)

  1. Competitor digital products and platformization are becoming the main product, not a side tool. Amazon DV/InterLink/TÜV Fast Track are platform-direct compliance channels; LRQA EiQ, Inspectorio SCNI, SGS FoodNexus, Sourcemap, and TradeBeyond/Pivot88 sell supply chain risk, quality, and compliance SaaS directly.[S2][S6][S10][S11]
  2. AI has three sellable landing zones: supply chain risk intelligence (EiQ/Inspectorio/Sourcemap), AI governance audits (Intertek AI², BV×AWS), and field/report efficiency (Checkfirst, CTI).
  3. QIMA’s direct competitors are not only traditional labs. SafetyCulture, The Inspection Company, Checkfirst, and IFS Cloud compete for “inspection/audit workflow digitization” budget, affecting QIMAone and traditional inspection.[S15][S16][S17][S19]
  4. Top sales signal this week: platforms require verifiable, traceable, system-ready compliance data; QIMA should package certificates, reports, photos, supplier, and order data into customer-system-ready evidence packs.

QIMA Implications

Product actions (PM)

  1. Amazon DV / MYC Readiness Pack: Build DV field checklists for toys, lithium/portable power, furniture/CSU, and laser products; TRF status tracking; report validity and standard-version alerts. Commit only to “result/certificate direct submission,” not LIMS raw-data auto-sync.
  2. CPSC eFiling Data Pack: Output CPC/GCC certificate fields, SKU/PO/importer data, lab credentials, product images, and test reports as CSV/API-ready payloads aligned with InterLink 2.0-style competitor messaging.[S2]
  3. ESG Supplier Risk Lite: In QIMAone, add supplier segmentation, audit history, country/category risk, and adverse media flags as a lightweight EiQ/Sourcemap alternative.
  4. OT / IoT Cyber Pre-check: For connected consumer goods, smart home, energy storage, and e-mobility accessory suppliers, offer IEC 62443/CRA/NIS2 pre-assessment checklists and referral paths to certification partners.
  5. Inspection AI Copilot: Use AI internally first for photo gap alerts, report consistency checks, certificate field extraction, and scheduling suggestions — with human final judgment, aligned with TIC Council/IAF HITL and ICT boundaries.[S23][S24]

Sales actions (business teams)

  • For Amazon sellers: Sell a closed loop around “listing stays live”: sample/report intake, standard-version review, DV submission, status feedback.
  • For retail brands: Counter-position QIMAone against Inspectorio/TradeBeyond: quality, compliance, traceability, and supplier risk should not live in Excel, email, and PDFs.
  • For ESG/procurement: Turn CSDDD/CSRD from advisory talk into an execution pack: supplier mapping + risk ranking + field audit + CAPA evidence.
  • For industrial/electronics customers: Use Kiwa×Siemens/BV cyber examples to show cybersecurity compliance entering procurement gates and product launch workflows.

Market / Regulation Trends

TIC Council / Global ACI (formerly IAF/ILAC): AI can enter conformity assessment, but accountability, audit trails, and human oversight remain mandatory

TIC Council’s 2026 digitalisation materials state that AI, IoT, digital twins, and digitally collected evidence can improve conformity-assessment efficiency, but independence, impartiality, technical competence, confidentiality, and Human-in-the-Loop cannot be removed. IAF MD 4:2025 treats AI as ICT usable in conformity assessment, but requires risk identification, data security, and assessment integrity. Since 2026, IAF/ILAC have merged into Global Accreditation Cooperation Incorporated; accreditation references should transition accordingly.[S23][S24]

Market research snapshot: sizing varies, direction consistently points to ESG, cyber, and AI-enabled assurance

MarketsandMarkets’ 2026 press-release summary estimates global TIC at ~$254.4B in 2026 and ~$306.1B by 2031 (CAGR 3.8%); Mordor’s estimate is ~$275.6B in 2026 and ~$345.4B by 2031 (CAGR 4.62%). These L8 sources are directional only and cannot replace competitor product news; free pages are summary/press-release caliber with paid full reports unavailable.[S25][S26]

CPSC eFiling

CPSC eFiling digitizes certificate submission to CPSC/CBP; Intertek explicitly supports CSV/API paths via InterLink 2.0 — a near-term QIMA consumer-certificate data productization opportunity.[S2][S4]

CRA / NIS2 / IEC 62443

TIC Council urges EU member states to advance NIS2 implementation; Kiwa, BV, TÜV, and others package IEC 62443, ISO 27001, CRA/NIS2 services — showing cyber moving from IT compliance into product and supply-chain gates.[S8][S9][S23]

IAF / Global ACI transition

Global ACI effective 2026-01-01 absorbs former IAF MLA and ILAC MRA scopes into a single MRA. QIMA/CNAS/CNCA acceptance narratives should gradually shift from “IAF/ILAC” to “Global ACI.”[S24]
