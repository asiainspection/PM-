Advance Payment Relaunch — Implementation Proposal

Data-driven, two-track segmentation to re-introduce Advance Payment following the unsuccessful July 2025 pilot (164 accounts). Prepared for Alexis Fabre Ringborg, VP Product.

Executive Summary

Root Cause (2025)
Segmentation used revenue only, no DSO filter — good payers were forced into Advance Payment, no escalation path, bundled with a price increase.
New Approach
New-customer default is the primary track and proceeds regardless. Existing-account conversion (DSO-filtered) is a secondary track — go/no-go decided in Phase 1 based on Phase 0 data and Sales buy-in.
Timeline
Go-live by Sep 4, 2026; monitoring runs continuously afterward.

Phase 1 — Define Standard & Escalation Design

Owner: Eric Wang · Sign-off: Alexis
Why 3 weeks: in the 2025 test, Sales/SIC sign-off on the account list alone took over 2 weeks — only 46 of 200 accounts had cleared by day 11. This phase adds a DSO-threshold decision and a new escalation-authority negotiation with Sales on top of that, so 3 weeks is sized with a buffer against the same slippage.
  • Decide DSO threshold based on Phase 0 data
  • Decide new-customer default scope — all new C&D clients, or a defined sub-segment
  • Design the two escalation mechanisms (Section below) and confirm authority levels with Sales
  • Go/no-go on the existing-customer track: proceed only if Phase 0 data shows a meaningful high-DSO population and Sales is willing to support it. The new-customer track proceeds regardless of this decision
2
Aug 17 – Sep 4, 2026 (3 weeks)

Phase 2 — Build & Execute

Owner: Eric Wang
Why 3 weeks: in 2025, batch execution was repeatedly cut short by SIC readiness — Batch 1 dropped from 107 to 57 accounts, Batch 2 from a 200 target to 164, partly due to holidays. 3 weeks gives room for readiness slippage without cutting scope again.
  • Implement system logic: in-scope new customers default to Advance Payment at signup; override restricted to Sales Director level and above, logged automatically
  • Only if the existing-customer track was approved in Phase 1: finalize target list (DSO above agreed threshold), batch-convert, and send customer notification
3
From Sep 7, 2026 — ongoing, weekly review

Phase 3 — Monitor & Iterate

Owner: Eric Wang · First review: Fri, Sep 11, then every week
  • Dashboard metrics: adoption rate, DSO/AR days trend, complaint volume, escalation/override volume
  • Qualitative: Sales feedback on whether Advance Payment is blocking new deals
  • Each weekly review decides: tighten, loosen, or hold the threshold/scope

Escalation Mechanism

A. New-Customer Pushback (Sales-side)

1
Sales RepFlags customer objection to Advance Payment at signing.
2
Sales DirectorReviews the case; has sole authority to grant a one-off override for that account. Normal reps cannot self-override.

B. Existing-Customer Batch Conversion (Sales-managed whitelist)

1
Sales RepReviews the batch list of accounts crossing the DSO threshold; flags any account they consider a relationship risk.
2
Sales DirectorDecides directly — either whitelist the account before the batch update runs, or revert an already-converted account back off Advance Payment. No PM approval needed; decision is final.

Benefits

BenefitWhy it matters
BenefitPreserves relationships with good-paying existing customers — the exact group that drove the 2025 backlash
BenefitNew-customer default is a one-time config change with a lasting, compounding cash-flow benefit — every new C&D client signed from go-live onward pays in advance
BenefitClear, Sales-owned escalation control avoids the reactive chaos of 2025 — no PM bottleneck, decisions stay with the people closest to the client
BenefitLow dev cost — this is a configuration and communication-driven initiative, not a build
