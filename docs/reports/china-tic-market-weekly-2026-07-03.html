平台直连Amazon DV / IntertekMYC + CPSC eFiling

Amazon DV and CPSC eFiling are turning certificates into platform-submitted compliance data

Who: Amazon-approved TIC providers including SGS, Intertek, Eurofins, CQC, UL Solutions and others; Intertek is also commercializing InterLink 2.0 for CPSC eFiling. Product: Amazon Direct Validation digital compliance channel, plus InterLink 2.0 for digitized CPC/GCC certificate data and CSV/API-ready eFiling with the CPSC Product Registry. Scenario: Sellers in toys, furniture/CSU, lithium batteries, power banks and related high-risk categories can no longer rely on self-uploaded compliance files; they initiate requests in Seller Central/MYC, select an approved TIC and the TIC submits results directly to Amazon. QIMA relevance: This is a direct competitive battleground for consumer-product labs: sales should position QIMA around reliable evidence intake, TRF/status tracking and direct-result submission, without claiming raw LIMS/sample-image synchronization unless proven.[S1][S2][S3][S4]

Executive Summary

  1. Competitor digitization is becoming the product, not the back office. Amazon DV/InterLink, LRQA EiQ, BV AITrack, Inspectorio SCNI, SGS FoodNexus, TradeBeyond/Pivot88 and Sourcemap all sell data workflows, risk intelligence or platform evidence rather than only lab capacity.[S1][S6][S8][S15]
  2. AI is monetized in three lanes: supply-chain risk intelligence (EiQ, Inspectorio, Sourcemap, TradeBeyond), AI-governance assurance (Intertek AI², BV x AWS, TÜV Rheinland ISO/IEC 42001) and inspection/lab productivity (Checkfirst, CTI).
  3. QIMA's direct competitors include SaaS and inspection platforms, not only TIC labs. SafetyCulture, The Inspection Company, Checkfirst, IFS Cloud and IFS Supply Chain Solutions compete for workflow digitization budgets that can precede or replace third-party inspection spend.[S19][S20][S21][S26]
  4. The near-term buyer demand is audit-ready, platform-consumable evidence. Sales teams should frame QIMA around direct-submission readiness, API/CSV-ready certificate fields, photo/report integrity, supplier risk triggers and CAPA evidence packs.

QIMA Implications

Product actions for PM

  1. Amazon DV / MYC readiness pack: standardize category-specific intake for toys, lithium batteries, power banks, laser products, furniture/CSU and e-mobility accessories; include TRF ID, standards/version checks, report validity and submission-status tracking.
  2. CPSC eFiling data pack: make CPC/GCC certificate data, importer/product identifiers, lab evidence and supporting files exportable in CSV/API-ready formats, matching InterLink-style messaging.[S2]
  3. ESG supplier risk lite: add supplier segmentation, country/category risk, audit history, adverse-media flags and CAPA progress into QIMAone before attempting a full EiQ/Sourcemap competitor build.
  4. OT / IoT cyber pre-check: offer an IEC 62443/CRA/NIS2 readiness checklist for connected consumer products, energy-storage devices, smart-home goods and e-mobility accessories.
  5. Inspection AI copilot: test AI for missing-photo checks, report consistency, certificate field extraction and scheduling recommendations; retain human final judgment in line with TIC Council's human-in-the-loop principle.[S13]

Sales actions for business teams

  • For Amazon sellers: sell the outcome as listing continuity: sample/report intake, standards review, TIC submission and status feedback.
  • For retailers and brands: position QIMAone against Inspectorio/TradeBeyond as the place where quality, compliance, traceability and supplier risk connect to independent field evidence.
  • For ESG/procurement: turn CSRD/CSDDD from advisory language into an execution bundle: supplier mapping, risk ranking, audit trigger, CAPA and evidence archive.
  • For industrial/electronics clients: use Kiwa x Siemens and BV Cybersecurity examples to show that cyber compliance is entering product launch and supplier approval gates.

Market / Regulation Trends

TIC Council / IAF: AI can support assurance, but human accountability remains central

TIC Council's 2026 materials call for trusted assurance as AI governance fragments globally and outline a framework for responsible digitalization of assurance services. The council supports AI, IoT, digital twins and data-driven assurance, while keeping independence, impartiality, competence, confidentiality and human oversight as non-negotiable. IAF/ILAC's transition into Global Accreditation Cooperation in 2026 and IAF's AIQI participation show that accreditation infrastructure is also moving toward AI assurance and global recognition.[S13][S14]

L8 market research: numbers differ, but all point toward ESG, cyber and AI-enabled assurance

MarketsandMarkets' March 2026 summary estimates the TIC market at USD 254.41bn in 2026 and USD 306.13bn by 2031, CAGR 3.8%. Mordor's 2026 report uses a higher 2026 base of USD 275.56bn and USD 345.39bn by 2031, CAGR 4.62%. These are free summaries/press-style pages with different scopes, so use them only as directional appendix evidence, not as competitor-news proof.[S30][S31]

CPSC eFiling

CPSC eFiling makes certificate data electronic for regulated consumer products; Intertek explicitly positions InterLink 2.0 for digitized CPC/GCC workflows and CSV/API routes.[S2]

CRA / NIS2 / IEC 62443

Cybersecurity compliance is moving from advisory to conformity-assessment pathways, especially for products with digital elements and industrial systems. BV, TÜV bodies and Kiwa/Siemens are all positioning around this demand.[S11][S12]

Accreditation continuity

Global Accreditation Cooperation's 2026 launch simplifies the former IAF/ILAC recognition architecture; this matters for cross-border acceptance of digital and AI-enabled conformity-assessment results.[S14]
