平台直连Amazon DV / Intertek / SGS / CQC / TÜV RheinlandSeller Central / MYC

Amazon Direct Validation is turning compliance upload into an approved-TIC data channel

Who: Amazon-approved TIC providers including SGS, Intertek, Eurofins, CQC and TÜV Rheinland. Product: A platform-direct DV compliance channel; Intertek also positions InterLink 2.0 as a digital certificate-data route for CPSC eFiling. Scenario: Sellers for toys, furniture/CSU, lithium batteries, power banks and other regulated categories must select an approved TIC in Seller Central/MYC; the TIC validates documents or tests samples and submits results directly to Amazon. Seller self-upload is no longer the accepted workflow in covered categories. QIMA relevance: This is a direct competitive lane for consumer goods testing: the sellable product is no longer only a test report, but report validity, field completeness, TRF status and platform acceptance. Executive Summary

  1. Competitor digital products are becoming the product, not a side tool. Amazon DV and InterLink are platform-direct compliance channels; LRQA EiQ, Inspectorio SCNI, SGS FoodNexus, Sourcemap and TradeBeyond/Pivot88 sell software layers around supplier risk, quality, compliance and evidence. [S1][S5][S9][S10]
  2. AI is monetized in three practical areas: supply-chain risk intelligence, AI governance assessment, and inspection/audit operations productivity.
  3. QIMA's direct competitive set now includes SaaS and inspection workflow platforms. Inspectorio, TradeBeyond/Pivot88, SafetyCulture, The Inspection Company, Checkfirst, Sourcemap and IFS compete for the same QA, compliance and supplier-risk budgets. [S12][S14][S15][S16]
  4. The strongest sales signal this week: buyers and platforms want compliance evidence that is structured, validated, traceable and system-consumable. PDF reports alone are becoming insufficient.

QIMA Implications

Product actions for PM

  1. Amazon DV / MYC readiness pack: Build category checklists for toys, lithium batteries, power banks and furniture/CSU: standards, report validity windows, lab accreditation, TRF ID tracking, model/SKU mapping and pass/fail status. Only claim direct result/certificate submission when QIMA has explicit channel evidence.
  2. CPSC eFiling data pack: Make CPC/GCC certificate data exportable in CSV/API-ready formats, including importer, product, manufacturer, testing date/location, lab contact and applicable rules. This directly counters Intertek InterLink messaging. [S2]
  3. ESG supplier risk lite: Add supplier segmentation, audit history, country/category risk and adverse-media flags to QIMAone before building a full EiQ/Sourcemap competitor.
  4. OT / IoT cyber pre-check: Offer an IEC 62443/CRA/NIS2 readiness checklist for connected consumer products, smart home devices, e-mobility accessories and light industrial suppliers.
  5. Inspection AI copilot: Pilot AI for missing-photo detection, report consistency checks, certificate field extraction and scheduler recommendations, with human final judgment and audit trail.

Sales actions for business teams

  • For Amazon sellers: Sell listing continuity: sample/report intake, standards/version review, TIC DV submission support, and status visibility.
  • For retail brands: Use Inspectorio/TradeBeyond language to reposition QIMAone: quality, compliance, traceability and supplier risk should not live in Excel, email and disconnected PDFs.
  • For ESG/procurement buyers: Convert CSDDD/CSRD from consulting into execution: supplier mapping, risk ranking, onsite audit, CAPA and exportable evidence packs.
  • For industrial/electronics clients: Use Kiwa × Siemens and BV cyber examples to show that cyber compliance is entering procurement and go-to-market gates.

Market / Regulation Trends

TIC Council and IAF: cyber and accreditation remain the trust backbone

TIC Council's latest cybersecurity positions call for faster, harmonized NIS2 implementation and recognition of ISO/IEC 27001 certification; its Cybersecurity Act 2 recommendations also reference future schemes for AI systems and supply-chain cybersecurity. Separately, IAF/ILAC have transitioned into Global Accreditation Cooperation Incorporated as of 1 January 2026, creating a single MRA framework for accredited conformity assessment recognition. [S22][S23]

L8 market research: use only as directional background

MarketsandMarkets' 2026 TIC market release estimates the global TIC market at USD 254.41B in 2026 and USD 306.13B by 2031, CAGR 3.8%. The source is a market-research summary/press-style page, so the figure is directional only. Its trend language is consistent with this week's competitor evidence: digital reporting, connected labs, remote audits, ESG assurance, supply-chain due diligence and AI/automation. [S24]

CPSC eFiling

Intertek states that InterLink 2.0 digitizes certificate data and supports CSV/API routes into the CPSC Product Registry, making consumer-product certificate data a near-term productization opportunity. [S2]

NIS2 / CRA / IEC 62443

Kiwa, BV Cybersecurity and TIC Council sources all point to IEC 62443 and ISO 27001 as practical evidence anchors for cyber compliance, even when the legal obligations differ by framework. [S7][S8][S22]

Accreditation transition

IAF Outlook reports Global Accreditation Cooperation Incorporated began full operations on 1 January 2026, combining ILAC and IAF functions. This matters for QIMA because digital/AI assurance still needs recognized accreditation pathways. [S23]
